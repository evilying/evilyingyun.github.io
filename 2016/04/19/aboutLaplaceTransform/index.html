Laplace Transform

  • Lessons

Laplace Transform

  • Laplace Transform in CT is Corresponding to $Z$ Transform in DT.

  • Laplace transform maps a function of time $t$ to a function of $s$
    $
    X(s) = \int x(t)e^{-st}dt
    $

    • Unilateral
      $
      X(s) = \int_0^{\infty} x(t)e^{-st}dt
      $
    • Bilateral
      $
      X(s) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x(t)e^{-st}dt
      $

  • ROC

    • Left-sided signals have left-sided Laplace transforms (bilateral only)
    • Example: $x(t) = -e^{-t}u(-t)$
      • $X(s) = \int_{-\infty}^{0}-e^{-t}e^{-st}dt = \cfrac{1}{s+1} $
      • Provided $Re(s+1) < 0$, then $Re(s) < -1$
    • Laplace transforms of left- and right-sided exponentials have the same form (except −); with left- and right-sided ROCs, respectively.
    • Laplace transform of a signal that is both-sided is a vertical strip
  • Solving Differential Equations with Laplace Transform
    • $\dot{y}(t) + y(t) = \delta{t}$
    • Laplace Transform of a Derivative
      • $y(t) = \dot{x}(t)$
      • $Y(s) = \mathcal{L{y(t)}} = sX(s)$
      • Provided $X(s)$ must converge.
    • Laplace Transform of the Impulse Function
      • $x(t) = \delta{t}$
      • $X(s) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x(t)e^{-st}dt = 1$
      • Sifting property:
        • Multiplying $f(t)$ by $\delta{t}$ and integrating over $t$ sifts out $f(0)$
    • Laplace transform converts a differential equation $\dot{y}(t) + y(t) = \delta{t}$ to an equivalent algebraic equation $sY + Y = 1$
    • Take inverse Laplace Transform(by recognizing form of transform): $y(t) = e^{-t}u(t), or\, y(t) = -e^{-t}u(-t), for\, each\, ROC$
  • Properties of Laplace Transform

Euler Approximation

leaky tank example

  • differential equation
    $\tau\dot{y}(t) = x(t) - y(t)$
  • system functional
    $\cfrac{Y}{X} = \cfrac{A}{A+\tau}$
  • system function
    $H(s) = \cfrac{Y(s)}{X(s)} = \cfrac{1}{1+\tau s}$
  • impulse response
    $h(t) = \cfrac{1}{\tau}e^{-t/\tau}u(t)$

Approximate continuous signals by discrete signals:
$x_d[n] = x_c(nT)$
$y_d[n] = y_c(nT)$

Forward Euler Approximation

Approximate derivative at $t = nT$ by looking forward in time:
$\dot{y_c}(nT) = \cfrac{y_d[n+1] - y_d[n]}{T}$
Approximate leaky-tank system using forward Euler approach
$y_d[n+1] - (1 - \cfrac{T}{\tau})y_d[n] = \cfrac{T}{\tau}x_d[n]$
Take the X transform:
$zY_d(z) - (1-\cfrac{T}{\tau})Y_d(z) = \cfrac{T}{\tau}X_d(z)$
DT Pole at $z = 1-\cfrac{T}{\tau}$

Dependence of DT pole on Stepsize

  • The CT pole is fixed($s = -\cfrac{1}{\tau}$), DT pole is changing
  • Dependence of DT pole on T is generic property of forward Euler
  • $z = 1-\cfrac{T}{\tau} = 1 + sT$
  • DT stability: CT pole must be inside circle of radius $\cfrac{1}{T}$ at $s = -\cfrac{1}{T}$
    • Because the $ROC$ of the DT stability is one circle, so the CT $ROC$ is also one circle.

Backward Euler Approximation

Approximate derivative at $t = nT$ by looking backward in time:
$\dot{y_c}(nT) = \cfrac{y_d[n] - y_d[n-1]}{T}$
